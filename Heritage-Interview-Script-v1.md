# HERITAGE – Family Voice & Story Session (v1)

## Intro (you say)
"Hi, this is a relaxed conversation.  
We're recording your voice and your stories so your family can hear you for generations.  
There are no right answers. Take your time, we can pause whenever you like."

---

## Section A – Warm-Up & Voice Variety

**A1 – Basics**
1. Please say your full name.
2. What do people call you? (nicknames, family names)
3. When and where were you born?

**A2 – Short Reads (for clarity)**
Ask them to read these slowly and naturally:

1. "Today is a good day to remember where I came from."
2. "My family means more to me than anything else."
3. "If you're hearing this in the future, I'm glad my voice is still here."

**A3 – Simple Sequences**
1. Count from 1 to 20.
2. Say the days of the week.
3. Say the months of the year.

(optional fun)
4. "She sells seashells by the seashore."
5. "Red leather, yellow leather."

---

## Section B – Life & Family Tree

**B1 – Parents & Roots**
1. Tell me about your parents. What were they like?
2. What's one memory of your childhood home?
3. Is there a story about your family that gets told over and over?

**B2 – Love & Relationships**
4. How did you meet your partner / spouse?
5. What was your first impression of them?
6. What's a hard moment you got through together?

**B3 – Work & Purpose**
7. What kind of work have you done in your life?
8. What are you most proud of in your work or creativity?
9. If you could go back and give your younger self advice about work, what would you say?

**B4 – Family Today**
10. Describe each of your children / close family in a sentence or two.
11. What traditions or habits make your family feel like "your" family?

---

## Section C – Messages to the Future

**C1 – Direct Messages**
1. If your great-grandchildren are listening to this, what do you want them to know about you?
2. What do you hope your family remembers about how you lived?

**C2 – Advice & Lessons**
3. What helped you get through your hardest times?
4. What advice would you give someone who feels lost or discouraged?

**C3 – Closing Line**
Finish this sentence in your own words:

> "If you're hearing my voice right now, I want you to remember that…"

Pause. Let them add anything else they want.

---

## Session Close (you say)
"Thank you for sharing all of this.  
Your voice and stories are now part of our family's living memory."
