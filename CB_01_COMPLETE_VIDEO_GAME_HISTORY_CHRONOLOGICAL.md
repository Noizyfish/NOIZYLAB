## üîå **ERA 0: PRE-HISTORY - BEFORE VIDEO GAMES (1940-1961)**

### **1940 - THE VERY BEGINNING**
- **Nim Computer** (Edward Condon)
  - Westinghouse display at World's Fair
  - Played mathematical game "Nim"
  - Computer won 90% of time
  - **FIRST computer game ever**

### **1947 - Cathode Ray Tube Amusement Device**
- Thomas T. Goldsmith Jr. and Estle Ray Mann
  - Patent filed (not built commercially)
  - Missile simulation using analog circuitry
  - Technically first "electronic game" concept

### **1950 - Programming Groundwork**
- **Claude Shannon** - Published "Programming a Computer for Playing Chess"
  - Laid theoretical foundation for game AI
  - Influenced all future computer game development

### **1952 - FIRST GRAPHICAL COMPUTER GAME**
- **OXO** (Noughts and Crosses)
  - Alexander S. Douglas, University of Cambridge
  - Ran on EDSAC computer
  - Tic-tac-toe against computer
  - Used CRT display
  - **FIRST game with visual display**

### **1958 - FIRST VIDEO GAME?**
- **Tennis for Two** (William Higinbotham)
  - Brookhaven National Laboratory
  - Displayed on oscilloscope
  - Side-view tennis game
  - Analog computer (not digital)
  - Created for public exhibition
  - Only ran for 2 years, then dismantled

### **1961-1962 - SPACEWAR! THE FIRST TRUE VIDEO GAME**
- **Spacewar!** (Steve Russell, MIT)
  - **THIS IS THE REAL BEGINNING**
  - Ran on PDP-1 minicomputer ($120,000 machine!)
  - Two spaceships battle around gravitational star
  - Hyperspace button, torpedoes, gravity physics
  - **FIRST game with:**
    - Two-player versus mode
    - Real-time action
    - Distributed to other institutions
  - Influenced EVERYTHING that followed
  - **Foundation of video game industry**

## üïπÔ∏è **ERA 1: BIRTH OF INDUSTRY (1971-1977)**

### **1971 - FIRST COMMERCIAL VIDEO GAME**
- **Computer Space** (Nolan Bushnell & Ted Dabney)
  - Based on Spacewar!
  - **FIRST arcade video game ever sold**
  - Manufactured by Nutting Associates
  - Fiberglass cabinet (futuristic design)
  - 1,500 units produced
  - Commercial failure (too complex)
  - BUT: Launched careers of Bushnell/Dabney
  - Led directly to Atari

### **1972 - THE GAME THAT CHANGED EVERYTHING**
- **PONG** (Atari - Nolan Bushnell, Allan Alcorn)
  - ‚≠ê **FIRST COMMERCIALLY SUCCESSFUL VIDEO GAME**
  - Table tennis simulation
  - Simple: Two paddles, one ball
  - Installed in Andy Capp's Tavern, California
  - So popular the coin mechanism jammed from overflow!
  - 19,000 Pong machines sold
  - **STARTED THE VIDEO GAME INDUSTRY**
  - Sparked hundreds of clones
  - Made "video game" a household concept

**Also 1972:**
- **Magnavox Odyssey** - First home video game console
  - Ralph Baer (father of home video games)
  - No CPU, analog circuitry
  - Plastic overlays on TV screen
  - Bundled with "light gun"

### **1973-1975 - ARCADE EXPANSION**
- Atari releases: Space Race, Pong Doubles, Quadrapong
- **Gun Fight** (1975) - Taito
  - First game with CPU (Intel 8080)
  - Human-like characters
- Home Pong consoles flood market

### **1976 - CARTRIDGE REVOLUTION**
- **Fairchild Channel F**
  - **FIRST console with ROM cartridges**
  - Changed industry forever
  - Games could be swapped!
  - CPU: Fairchild F8

- **Breakout** (Atari - Steve Wozniak, Steve Jobs)
  - Paddle + brick breaking
  - Later influenced Apple computer design

### **1977 - ATARI 2600 LAUNCHES**
- **Atari Video Computer System (VCS/2600)**
  - ROM cartridge system
  - Sold 30+ million units
  - Defined home gaming for decade
  - Launch titles: Combat, Air-Sea Battle
