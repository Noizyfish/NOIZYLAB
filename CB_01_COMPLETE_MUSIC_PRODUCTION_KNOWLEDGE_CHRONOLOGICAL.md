## üéµ **ERA 1: BIRTH OF ELECTRONIC MUSIC (1920-1959)**

### **1920 - The Beginning**
- **Theremin** (L√©on Theremin) - FIRST electronic musical instrument
  - Used electromagnetic fields controlled by hand proximity
  - Featured in "The Day the Earth Stood Still" soundtrack
  - Still used today in experimental music

### **1935 - Electromechanical Era**
- **Hammond Organ** (Laurens Hammond)
  - Tonewheel technology
  - Became staple in jazz, gospel, rock
  - Still manufactured and used today

### **1956 - First Programmable Synthesizer**
- **RCA Mark II Sound Synthesizer**
  - Developed at Columbia-Princeton Electronic Music Center
  - Programmed with punch cards
  - Used by pioneering electronic composers

## üéπ **ERA 2: ANALOG SYNTHESIS REVOLUTION (1960-1979)**

### **1964 - Voltage Control Revolution**
- **Moog Modular Synthesizer** (Robert Moog)
  - REVOLUTIONARY: First voltage-controlled synthesizer
  - Modular design with patch cables
  - Used by Wendy Carlos ("Switched-On Bach" 1968)
  - Defined electronic music sound for decades

### **1970 - Semi-Modular Classic**
- **ARP 2600** (ARP Instruments)
  - More affordable semi-modular synth
  - Used extensively in film scores
  - Still emulated in modern software

### **1970s - Polyphonic Revolution**
- **Sequential Circuits Prophet-5** (1977)
  - First fully programmable polyphonic synthesizer
  - Preset storage via microprocessor
  - Defined 80s pop/rock sound

- **Oberheim** - Four Voice, OB-X series
- **Yamaha CS-80** (1976) - Blade Runner soundtrack
- **Roland Jupiter-8** (1981) - Lush analog classic

### **1975 - Digital Effects Pioneer**
- **Eventide H910 Harmonizer**
  - First digital pitch shifter
  - Revolutionary in studio production

### **1978 - FIRST DIGITAL AUDIO WORKSTATION**
- **Soundstream Digital Editing System**
  - Used DEC PDP-11/60 minicomputer
  - First hard disk digital audio recording
  - Beginning of DAW era

### **1979 - Digital Sampling Arrives**
- **Fairlight CMI (Computer Musical Instrument)**
  - First digital sampler/workstation
  - $25,000-$30,000 (extremely expensive)
  - Page R: First graphical audio sequencer
  - Used by Peter Gabriel, Kate Bush, Herbie Hancock
  - REVOLUTIONARY: Changed music production forever

---

## üíæ **ERA 3: DIGITAL REVOLUTION (1980-1989)**

### **1980-1987 - Early DAW Development**
- **Lucasfilm SoundDroid** (1980-1987)
  - Designed for film post-production
  - Real-time multichannel EQ and mixing
  - Pioneered nonlinear audio editing

### **1981-1983 - MIDI Revolution**
- **MIDI Standard** (Musical Instrument Digital Interface - 1983)
  - Allowed electronic instruments to communicate
  - CHANGED EVERYTHING: Universal control protocol
  - Enabled computer-based music production

### **1983 - FM Synthesis Goes Mainstream**
- **Yamaha DX7**
  - First mass-market digital synthesizer
  - FM (Frequency Modulation) synthesis
  - 160,000+ units sold
  - Defined 80s sound: bells, electric pianos, bass
  - Most successful synthesizer in history (units sold)

### **1984 - Early DAW Systems**
- **Fairlight CMI Series III**
  - Page R graphical sequencer
  - First true DAW interface

- **WaveFrame AudioFrame**
  - Digital audio workstation
  - Combined sampler, recorder, mixer

### **1985 - Affordable Sampling**
- **Ensoniq Mirage**
  - First affordable sampler ($1,695)
  - Made sampling accessible to everyone

### **1986-1989 - Hip-Hop Production Tools**
- **Akai MPC60** (1988) - Roger Linn design
  - Combined sampler + sequencer + drum pads
  - LEGENDARY: Defined hip-hop production
  - Used by J Dilla, DJ Premier, Pete Rock

- **E-mu SP-1200** (1987)
  - 12-bit sampler with gritty sound
  - Hip-hop classic (Bomb Squad, Large Professor)

### **1987 - LA Synthesis**
- **Roland D-50**
  - Linear Arithmetic synthesis (samples + synthesis)
  - Lush pads and textures
  - Defined late 80s/early 90s sound

### **1988 - Best-Selling Synth Ever**
- **Korg M1**
  - Most successful synth by revenue ($300M+)
  - Workstation with samples, sequencer, effects
  - "M1 Piano" sound everywhere in 90s pop

### **1989 - Software DAW Era Begins**
- **Digidesign Sound Tools**
  - Precursor to Pro Tools
  - First affordable software DAW
  - 2-channel digital recording for Mac

- **Steinberg Cubase** (1989)
  - Started as MIDI sequencer
  - Would become major DAW platform

## üíø **ERA 4: GOLDEN AGE - DIGITAL PRODUCTION EXPLODES (1990-1999)**

### **1990 - Early Sample CD Libraries**
- **Zero-G Datafile Series** - First sample CDs
- **Time+Space ProSamples** - Early CD collections
- **E-mu Sound Library CD-ROMs** - Hardware sampler libraries

### **1991 - PRO TOOLS ARRIVES**
- **Digidesign Pro Tools**
  - INDUSTRY STANDARD established
  - TDM (Time Division Multiplexing) DSP system
  - Became standard for professional studios worldwide

### **1992 - Plugin Revolution Begins**
- **Waves Audio** founded
  - Q10 Parametric EQ (first plugin)
  - L1 Limiter (revolutionary)
  - Started plugin industry

- **Magix Samplitude** - Windows DAW
- **Steinberg Cubase Audio** - Added audio recording to MIDI

### **1993 - Professional Windows DAW**
- **SAWStudio**
  - First Windows-based professional DAW
  - Cost-effective alternative to Pro Tools

### **1994 - Legendary Sample Libraries**
- **Spectrasonics Distorted Reality**
  - Sound design library
  - Eric Persing (legendary sound designer)

### **1996 - VST STANDARD CHANGES EVERYTHING**
- **Steinberg VST (Virtual Studio Technology)**
  - Universal plugin standard
  - Opened floodgates for third-party plugins
  - REVOLUTIONARY: Anyone could develop plugins

- **Native Instruments** founded
  - Would become major player in software instruments

### **1997 - ASIO Protocol**
- **Steinberg ASIO** (Audio Stream Input/Output)
  - Low-latency audio driver protocol
  - Essential for real-time audio on Windows

- **FL Studio** (FruityLoops) released
  - Pattern-based sequencer
  - Became huge in hip-hop/electronic production

### **1998-1999 - Major Sample Libraries**
- **Akai S-series CD-ROMs** - S1000/S3000/S5000 libraries
- **Big Fish Audio** - Genre-specific libraries (Hip Hop City)
- **Sonic Foundry Acid** - Loop-based production libraries
- **Best Service XXL Series** - Massive drum loop collections
